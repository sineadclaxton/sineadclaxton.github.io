HAVE YOU HEARD OF ROMEO AND JULIET?

Have you studied it in school? Do you remember endlessly

highlighting paragraphs and the words eventually losing all

meaning?


Leave your bland school studies behind and let our unique

production of Romeo and Juliet with contempary dance bring

you on a new unexpected jouney.

HAVE YOU EVER BEEN IN LOVE?

With a person, a place or even a food? Do you remember

the feeling you had? How your heart skipped a beat,

how you felt alive, enegized, excited and surprised

all at the same time? Do you want to experience

that feeling again?

Shakespeare’s Romeo and Juliet is one of the oldest love

The reason it’s still relevant today is because it depicts how

life isn’t always perfect, how sometimes you have to fight

for what you believe in no matter the cost.

This production is a modern day marriage of two parallels.

Poetry and hip hop will interwine right in front of your eyes,

filling your mind with new memories of the real story of Romeo

and Juliet through dance.

About the Artists

Juliet


Sarah Hughes is an American actress and dancer.

She has been acting for the last five years. She

started out of the Netflix hit TV show Designated

Survivor. Since then she has worked with Cillian

Murphy and Brendan Gleeson.

Romeo


Barry Atkinson is a well known Irish actor.

His break-out performacewas on RTE’s

Normal People. Acting is in his blood; his

brother Stephen played a supporting role

in The Lobster with Colin Farrell. Barry is no

stranger to dance, he played Billy Elliot

in the first Broadway production.

The Director


Pablo Mancini is an Italian-based director.

His most notable movie production was

La La Land. Even though he works behind

the camera, he loves the limelight and has

had many appearances on Graham Norton

talking about the arts.
